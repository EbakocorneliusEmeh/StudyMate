Photosynthesis is the process plants use to convert light energy into chemical energy.

It mainly takes place in the chloroplasts of plant cells.

The reactants for photosynthesis are carbon dioxide, water, and light energy.

The products of photosynthesis are glucose and oxygen.

Chlorophyll is the green pigment that absorbs light, especially from the blue and red parts of the spectrum.

The general word equation for photosynthesis is:
carbon dioxide + water -> glucose + oxygen

Photosynthesis is important because it produces food for plants and releases oxygen into the atmosphere.
